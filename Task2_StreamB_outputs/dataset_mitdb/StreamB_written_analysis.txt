Stream B written analysis:
1. Motion artifacts are non-stationary, so their amplitude and frequency content change with time.
2. Fixed high-pass, notch, and low-pass filters are designed for stationary frequency bands and cannot fully remove sudden electrode-motion transients.
3. When a large transient enters the ECG, fixed filters can leave residual spikes or ringing.
4. The STFT shows artifact energy localized in time, unlike normal ECG energy which repeats with each beat.
5. A short STFT window improves time localization but gives poorer frequency resolution.
6. A long STFT window improves frequency resolution but smears sudden artifacts over time.
7. Frame-based energy/variance detection was used to identify corrupted segments.
8. Interpolation replaces corrupted samples using clean neighboring samples and works well for short artifacts.
9. Median replacement suppresses spikes but may distort waveform details if the window is too large.
10. The residual spike cleanup method first applies the Stream A filter and then replaces only detected transient leftovers.
11. Based on the quantitative metric available in this run, the best method was: Hybrid median/mean + filter.
12. For real-time use, frame detection plus interpolation is the simplest low-latency option; wavelet denoising is optional and toolbox-dependent.
